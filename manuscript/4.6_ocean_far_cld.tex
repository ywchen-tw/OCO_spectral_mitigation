\subsection{Ocean validation and far-cloud controls}
\label{sec:result-ocean_far_cldl}




Near-cloud ATom legs improve in median |residual| from 0.53 to 0.45 ppm, while the far-cloud date (9 October 2017) is a negative control that the correction leaves nearly unchanged (Fig.~\ref{fig:ocean-validation}a). Against the shipborne spectrometers, the correction collapses footprint scatter ($\sigma$ 0.63 → 0.27 ppm) without moving the clear-sky control day (22 June 2019); the residual ~+1 ppm absolute offset is dominated by reference-scale differences, not created by the correction (Fig.~\ref{fig:ocean-validation}b).
